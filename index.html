02 — The 3 hard problems underneath the surface
01
Data completeness ≠ data usability
Weflow captures activities reliably. But users are still building custom Salesforce flows to get basic fields like Last Email Date. The signal layer exists — the reporting layer doesn't follow through. Reps trust the capture; RevOps can't yet trust the output for forecasting without manual workarounds.
02
50 signals available. 3 signals actually used.
Weflow surfaces 50+ deal signals. But no filtering by account or project means reps scan the entire list manually. The signals are there — the prioritization layer is missing. The next hard problem isn't more signals. It's knowing which signals matter most for each team's motion.
03
Adoption breaks where behavior change is required
The Outlook plugin requires active installation per rep. When it's skipped, Last Open Date stops working. A product that depends on rep behavior change will always have adoption ceilings — especially in enterprise. The fix is zero-friction capture that works regardless of what reps do or don't install.
